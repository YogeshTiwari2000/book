Independent Reading and Viewing Suggestions

Choose additional reading and viewing to continue your exploration of the power of communication and the Essential Question.

Read (print and online books)
Suffragette: My Own Story, by Emmeline Pankhurst
Read this first-person account of the British fight for women’s suffrage from a leader of the movement in the United Kingdom.
American Born Chinese, by Gene Luen Yang
This graphic novel focuses on three characters to tell a story of growing up bicultural in Los Angeles, California, through text and images.
Charles, by Shirley Jackson
Written in 1948, this famous short story follows a young boy, Laurie, as he tells his parents nightly stories about “Charles,” who misbehaves at school. Will the parents (and the reader) learn who Charles really is?
On Writing, by Stephen King
From the master of horror fiction comes a memoir about his life work: writing. King tells interesting stories about his writing and coaches his readers on the written word.
View (videos and films)
nside Out, 2015
Watch this animated film in which feelings and emotions become the main characters as a young girl deals with her family’s move to California.
Coda, 2021
As the only hearing person in a deaf family, Ruby must make some difficult choices when her family’s business starts to experience difficulties.
E.T., 1982
In this classic film, a young boy meets a creature from outer space, an extraterrestrial, who he names E.T. Can the pair figure out how to get E.T. to “phone home” before it’s too late?
This Space Available, 2011
Take a ride through 11 cities around the world as the filmmakers explore how visual marketing affects individuals and communities.
