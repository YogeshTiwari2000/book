Learning from Data

Genre Case Study

A case study is an examination of a real-world scenario (situation). Case studies often include specific data about a particular situation or phenomenon. That data is then analyzed and used to answer questions or draw conclusions about the situation or phenomenon.

PURPOSE FOR READING You will read a case study from NASA involving space satellites. As you read, look closely at the table and graph, and notice the questions and answers based on the data. Analyze how effectively the author presents the questions and communicates the answers.

A

Read the case study about space satellites.

Exploring Space Mathematics

by NASA

1 Satellite technology is everywhere! Right now, there are over 1587 working satellites orbiting Earth. They represent over 60 billion in assets to the world’s economy. In the United States alone, satellites and the many services that they provide produce over 25 billion every year.
2 But satellites do not work forever. Typically, they have to be replaced every 10 to 15 years as new services are created, and better technology is developed. Satellites in the lowest orbits, called Low Earth Orbit (LEO), orbit between 300 to 1000 kilometers above the ground. Because Earth’s atmosphere extends hundreds of kilometers into space, LEO satellites eventually experience enough frictional drag from the atmosphere that at altitudes below 300 km, they fall back to Earth and burn up. The table shown gives the number of LEO satellites that re-entered Earth’s atmosphere, and the number of sunspots each year between 1969 and 2004.

GLOSSARY

satellites (n) objects that rotate a planet or star

orbiting (v) rotating or revolving around

assets (n) resources that have monetary or economic value

frictional drag (n) slowing down of a moving object due to forces such as air

altitudes (n) heights

sunspots (n) dark areas on the surface of the sun
