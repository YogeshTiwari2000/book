Tip!

Writing

When writing a persuasive speech or letter, it is important to consider your audience. Include facts, details, and examples that your audience will connect with. For example, consider your audience’s background knowledge to include any information they need to know. Consider your audience’s prior experiences to include related examples that will appeal to their emotions.

Some people say argue that social technology is good because it allows people who are not together to share each other’s lives through photos and stories. While this may be true in a few cases, I believe it has become a substitute for real interaction. So, I ask you now to stop doing this reverse this trend. B
Revise Revise your draft. Use the rubric, model student revision, and exemplars to help you.

Use this checklist:

Revision Checklist

  • Is my opinion or viewpoint clearly stated in the introduction?
  • Did I follow argument text structure, including a claim, evidence, a counterclaim, and a rebuttal?
  • Did I appeal to my audience’s logic, emotions, and credibility?
  • What can I add to the writing to be more persuasive?
  • Does anything need to be rearranged to be more persuasive?
  • Does my writing flow in a way that is easy to read?
  • Did I include citations for any reference materials I used?
C
Peer review Share your writing with a classmate and discuss. Ask your classmate:
  1. What did you like about my essay?
  2. Was there anything confusing or unclear?
  3. Is there anything else you want to know more about?
D
Revise again Based on the conversations you had during your peer review, make any final revisions.
